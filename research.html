Research

Our research focuses on understanding the unique barriers faced by refugees, non-EU residents, and those without traditional secondary education. By collaborating with students, educators, and community leaders, we gather valuable perspectives that inform the development of our platform.

Initial Research

Understanding the SDGs

Our journey began with a deep dive into the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly focusing on Goal 4 (Quality Education) and Goal 10 (Reduced Inequalities). We identified a significant gap in access to education for underserved communities.

Identifying the Challenges

Through research and interviews, we discovered barriers such as limited resources, language barriers, and lack of tailored educational support. These insights guided us toward building a solution that directly addresses these issues.

Collaborative Ideation

By collaborating with educators, students, and community leaders, we brainstormed ideas that could bridge these gaps. The feedback reinforced the need for a platform centered around inclusivity and accessibility.

Conceptualizing UnityHub

These findings culminated in the creation of UnityHub, a platform designed to provide accessible, tailored educational resources. It aligns with the SDGs by addressing education gaps and fostering inclusivity.

Final Topic Research

Research Overview

Our final topic research took a deep dive into the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), specifically focusing on Goal 4: Quality Education and Goal 10: Reduced Inequalities. These goals emphasize the urgent need to provide equitable, inclusive, and quality education to individuals from all walks of life, regardless of their socioeconomic, geographic, or cultural backgrounds. By examining the core principles and global benchmarks outlined in these SDGs, we aimed to understand the systemic barriers that prevent access to education for underserved communities. Through extensive analysis, we uncovered several recurring challenges, including limited access to digital resources, language barriers, and a lack of culturally relevant educational materials. We also explored disparities in educational funding and infrastructure across different regions, which disproportionately affect refugees, non-EU residents, and individuals without traditional secondary education.
